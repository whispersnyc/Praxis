1,000 True Fans

  • 1,000 True Fans Concept:
    • Creator needs 1,000 true fans to make a living.
    • True fan: buys anything you produce, drives long distances, purchases multiple versions, attends events.
    • Aim for direct connection with 1,000 true fans instead of stardom.
      • Direct relationship is key: fans pay you directly.
    • Goal: earn $100 profit/year from each true fan.
      • 1,000 fans x $100/fan = $100,000/year.
      • 1,000 is a feasible number compared to millions.
      • Adjust number based on earnings per fan, living expenses, team size.
      • Aim for one day's wages per year from each fan.
    • Regular fans exist around true fans, expanding income.
    • True fans are marketing force for regular fans.
  • New Technology Enables True Fan Model:
    • Peer-to-peer communication allows direct sales.
    • Creator keeps full payment, reducing needed fan count.
    • Obscure content is one click away from popular content.
    • Long Tail: Total sales of obscure items equal or exceed bestsellers.
    • Aggregators channel attention to rare creations.
    • Anything can interest at least one person in a million; find those fans.
    • Corporations are ill-suited for niche audiences.
  • Crowdfunding:
    • Fans finance your next product.
    • 2,000 crowdfunding platforms worldwide.
    • Kickstarter: largest crowdfunder, $2.5 billion raised, 241 funders average.
    • True fans will become Kickstarter funders.
  • Cultivating True Fans:
    • Time-consuming, requires ongoing skills.
    • Hire someone to deal with fans if needed.
    • Use direct relations with super fans in addition to intermediaries.
  • Original 2008 Essay Highlights:
    • Long tail is mixed blessing for creators, massive competition.
    • Convert Lesser Fans into True Fans.
    • Maintain direct contact with True Fans.
    • Technologies enable small-time manufacturing.
    • Young artists have another path other than stardom, a path made possible by the very technology that creates the long tail.
    • Linear increase in fans needed for creative groups.
    • Direct fans are best, indirect requires more fans.
    • Actual number of fans varies depending on media.
  • Related concepts:
    • Microcelebrities: famous to 1,500 people.
    • Micro-patronage, distributed patronage.
    • Street Performer Protocol: author goes directly to readers before publishing.
    • Jill Sobule: used fans to finance recording fees.
  • Caveats:
    • Not every artist is willing to nurture fans.
    • Poverty is the usual alternative.
